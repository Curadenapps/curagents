## What is iTOP?

iTOP (Individually Trained Oral Prophylaxis) is Curaden's systematic daily
biofilm management programme. It is clinician-led and patient-performed.

**Core principle:** Biofilm removal is a daily patient behaviour, not a
periodic clinical intervention. The clinician's role is to train, measure,
and motivate — not to clean on behalf of the patient.

**iTOP within the Curaden ecosystem:**
- BOB App provides the measurement layer (Bleeding on Brushing index)
- iTOP provides the methodology and training layer
- Curaden Academy provides the education and CPD layer

## Session Structure

### Session 1 — Initial Assessment

1. Establish baseline: BOB measurement (and PBE if available)
2. Identify problem areas: proximal vs. buccal bleeding, plaque distribution
3. Introduce iTOP concept to patient: daily self-care, individualised technique
4. Demonstrate correct toothbrush positioning and Modified Bass technique
5. Document baseline measurements and agreed intervention plan

### Session 2 — Technique Reinforcement (Follow-up)

1. Re-measure BOB (and PCR if PBE session)
2. Compare to baseline — show patient the trend
3. Correct any technique errors observed at Session 1
4. Introduce or reinforce interdental care (type and size matched to patient)
5. Adjust technique focus based on measurement results

### Re-check Session

1. Full BOB/PBE re-measurement
2. Longitudinal comparison (Session 1 vs. current)
3. Advance or consolidate technique based on trend
4. Set next recall interval based on outcome

## Brushing Techniques

### Modified Bass Technique (primary iTOP recommendation)

- Place toothbrush bristles at 45° to the gingival margin
- Apply gentle vibratory pressure directed into the sulcus
- Short horizontal strokes (2–3 teeth width)
- Complete buccal and lingual surfaces systematically
- Use soft or ultra-soft bristle brush

### Roll Technique (alternative — maintenance patients)

- Position brush at 45° to gingiva, bristles pointing apically
- Roll brush coronally in a sweeping motion
- Less effective sulcular penetration than Modified Bass
- Suitable for patients with established low-inflammation baseline

## Interdental Care

| Patient Profile | Recommended Aid | Notes |
|-----------------|----------------|-------|
| Tight contacts, no recession | Floss (waxed) | Demonstrate C-shape wrap technique |
| Moderate spacing | Interdental brush (ISO size matched) | Use without toothpaste unless fluoride protocol |
| Wide embrasures | Interdental brush or rubber-tip | Larger ISO size, irrigator as adjunct |
| Implants / fixed prosthetics | Superfloss or interdental brush | Avoid floss under pontic without clinician guidance |
| Low dexterity | Floss pick or water irrigator | Irrigator does not replace mechanical removal |

## Adjunct Products (Approved Reference List)

> Only recommend products from this list. Do not name specific brands not listed here
> without verification against the current Curaden approved product catalogue.

- **Toothpaste:** Fluoride-containing toothpaste ≥1000ppm F (standard adult)
- **Mouthrinse:** Chlorhexidine short-term (≤4 weeks) for acute inflammation;
  fluoride rinse for caries-active patients
- **Disclosing agents:** Erythrosine or two-tone disclosing tablets for
  visualisation at Session 1 and re-check

## Motivational Framing Guidelines

Use measurement-based framing only. Approved patterns:

| Approved | Not Approved |
|----------|-------------|
| "Your BOB score improved from X% to Y% — that means less bleeding when you brush at home" | "Your gums are healthier now" |
| "Plaque levels dropped from X% to Y% after brushing — that's the technique working" | "You've cured the problem" |
| "The goal is BOB below 10% — you're at X% today, which means [continue / adjust technique]" | "This will prevent gum disease" |

## Recall Intervals (Clinician Decision Guide)

| BOB% at re-check | Suggested recall | Notes |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| < 10% | 6–12 months | Maintenance — technique established |
| 10–20% | 3–6 months | Improvement — reinforce technique |
| > 20% | 1–3 months | Intensive — re-introduce full iTOP programme |

*Recall interval is a clinical decision. This table is a reference guide only.*
